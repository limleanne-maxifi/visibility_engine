Design Assessment

Should #152438 become the standard subpage background?

The lighter navy creates a clear visual layer between the homepage (most dramatic, marketing-led) and functional subpages (calmer, task-led). The header stays anchored at #091521 throughout, so the site never loses its dark-premium identity — only the content canvas shifts.

✓ Adopt

Tool & form pages

The AI Visibility Snapshot form, results page, and any future calculators or audits. Users are in task mode — a slightly softer background reduces visual fatigue across multiple steps without breaking brand context.

✓ Adopt

Insights / blog

Long-form reading on pure #0B1929 creates harsh contrast against white cards. The +10% lighter navy substantially improves text readability and sustained engagement — the single biggest gain from this change.

✓ Adopt

Legal & policy pages

Privacy policy, terms, cookie notice. Dense text pages benefit most from a lighter canvas. Already implemented with this background — consistent and appropriate.

⚠ Case by case

Service / feature pages

AEO, Engine, Work pages. If the page opens with a full-bleed hero section, keep that hero at #0B1929 for dramatic impact and switch to #152438 for the body content below the fold. Don't apply the lighter bg to the hero itself.

✗ Keep dark

Homepage

#0B1929 is the correct call for the homepage — it sets the brand's premium, technical authority. Lightening it would undermine the immediate first impression that distinguishes Maxifi from generic digital agencies.

✗ Keep dark

Case studies / Work

Results-led pages with full-bleed imagery and data visualisations perform better on the deeper navy — the darkness amplifies the visual weight of proof. Lighter background risks making the data feel less dramatic.

Proposed background rule

Homepage #0B1929 — darkest. Maximum brand impact.
Hero sections #0B1929 — always. Maintains entry drama on every page.
Tool / form / legal #152438 — task-focused, readable, still on-brand.
Insights body #152438 — best reading experience on dark sites.
Navigation header #091521 — always the darkest element. Visual anchor.
